## Problems we don't have

### Misleading expectations
**Most pack sites:** Show grail cards in marketing, hide the floor odds. You don't find out the typical pull is junk until after you spend.

**CatchBack:** Every pack tier publishes the full probability table — every bucket, every percentage, for every risk level — before you open. No surprises.

### "Average pull < pack cost" economics
**Most pack sites:** Bake their margin into the pack so the typical pull is worth less than what you paid. That's how they make money.

**CatchBack:** Our packs are priced so that **average pulled value ≈ pack price**. Open a $50 Silver pack, your average hit is around $50. We don't make money on the math being against you.

### Disappointing floor hits
**Most pack sites:** Floor pulls are filler cards no one wanted.

**CatchBack:** Floors are real cards from the published pool, with real market value. Even your worst pull is something you could actually sell.

### Take-it-or-leave-it buyback windows
**Most pack sites:** Buyback offers expire in minutes. You can't think it over, watch the market, or hold for upside.

**CatchBack:** Every card we sell comes with a **perpetual 80% buyback guarantee**. You can hold for as long as you want and cash out whenever you choose.

### Unverifiable randomness
**Most pack sites:** Black-box RNG. You can't audit fairness.

**CatchBack:** Every pull is seeded by your transaction ID and produced by a deterministic algorithm we publish. Same inputs always produce the same output — verifiable on every pull. See [Provably Fair](/about-catchback/provably-fair) for the technical details.

### Hidden fees, paid promotion
**Most pack sites:** Listing fees, final value fees, promoted listings, category penalties.

**CatchBack:** Flat $1 marketplace fee per party, no listing fees, no paid promotion. See [Marketplace](/about-catchback/marketplace).
